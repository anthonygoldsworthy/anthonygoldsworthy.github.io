UPCOMING ALBUMS


COWBOY CARTER, Beyoncé • RENAISSANCE was one of my most played albums of 2023, mostly because it typically takes a year or two for an entire album to grow on me. Very rarely do I enjoy an entire album top to bottom upon the first listen. Lemonade was one of those rare cases, and there were a couple things that surprised me about that. Up to that point, I'd written off country music, but "Daddy Lessons" was a standout in the tracklist, and it really gave me an appreciation for well-written country, because all I'd heard for the last 6 years was "country" that just sounds like Nickelback with a southern accent. COWBOY CARTER so far has been solid, and I'm really excited to see where else it'll go.

All Born Screaming, St. Vincent • St. Vincent has honestly never missed, and this is shaping up to be no exception. "Broken Man" is one of my favorite tracks of the year so far, and it's only just come out. St. Vincent is particularly good having "eras" as an artist, and it's not often that you see her level of commitment. Every project is a complete departure from the last and she's almost unrecognizable between album covers. I was excited to have a darker and more agressive tone on the lead single, because it really suits how I (and presumably many others) feel about life right now. Her previous release Daddy's Home was a standout album, and showed an even more introspective side, and featured some of my favorite instrumtals in recent memory. Could not be more excited for this one.

brat, Charli XCX • I won't claim to be a massive Charli XCX fan; I've got a handful of songs that I enjoy, but only one album that I've really connected with. Crash was really fantastic pop, and I was really surprised about how much I enjoy it. brat is headed in a very different direction, based on "Von Dutch" but it's on trend with the Y2K aesthetic obsession. So far it really feels promising.

Radical Optimism, Dua Lipa • Let me be clear! I hate this cover and I hate the name of the album, but I thoroughly enjoy Dua's recent work, and am excited to hear what this sounds like. Houdini is underrated. She's another fantastic "eras" person and she seems to be cranking out hits, so here's hoping.
